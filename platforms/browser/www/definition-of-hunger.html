Definition Of Hunger

According to different degree of severity, we can define hunger as belows:

Famine

Famine refers to severe food shortages and widespread, leading to large-scale population hunger, malnutrition and death.

Hunger

Hunger is used to describe the body does not absorb enough nutrients to sustain basic health conditions.

Food shortage

Food shortage, people unable to represent the physical, social and economic three, get enough food to maintain overall health and the necessities of life.

Acute malnutrition

Acute food shortages and malnutrition is usually caused by a sudden, and often food shortages caused by a drought or other natural disasters. The body will appear sharp thin alarm, underweight with height is not proportional. Children lack adequate food, the body can only consume the energy stored in fat, and ultimately lead to the collapse of bodily functions.

Chronic malnutrition

Children long failed to get enough nutrients, it will cause long-term malnutrition. Stunted his body siren, discrepancies too short height and age. Children with developmental delays, physical and brain development slower than normal, especially after the birth of one thousand days, this will cause permanent damage to a child's growth.

Malnutrition

Malnutrition not feel hungry, but to health in medicine. The body due to lack of food and nutrition and failed to maintain normal bodily functions. Malnutrition is divided into two categories: acute malnutrition and chronic malnutrition.
